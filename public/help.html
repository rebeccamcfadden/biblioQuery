About Us

BiblioQuery is a web-based app for Texas A&M students to search, buy, and sell textbooks. We use price searching APIs to help you find the textbooks you need at a reasonable price. We also make use of multiple book databases to provide cover images, previews, and other rich data to help you narrow your book search. In addition, we provide an avenue for A&M students to post textbooks and sell them to other A&M students. In this way, we can decrease book waste and use the Aggie Network to reduce textbook costs for students.

How does it work?

There are three main functions of the application: searching for books, posting books to sell, and communicating with other users. While posting books and communicating with other users requires a TAMU email to log in, searching is open to anyone without authentication.

Login

To login to biblioQuery, click the “Login with NetID” button in the upper-right of the screen. You will then be prompted to select a Google account to authenticate with. You must log in with a TAMU Google account to access biblioQuery. Once you have logged in, the app will ask you if you will allow push notifications. By selecting yes, you give biblioQuery permission to notify you when another user messages you through the chat. Background notifications will not work without this enabled.

Once logged in, you can view your biblioQuery profile by clicking the button in the upper right with your name and profile picture. You can view reviews left on your profile by other users, view your book listings, and edit your profile. To edit your profile, click the “edit profile” button underneath your profile picture. You can then edit any of the fields shown. To revert a field back to default or empty, simply clear the field. To save your changes, click the “save changes” button. To cancel, select “cancel changes”. To remove your profile picture and thereby restore it to its default value, select “remove profile picture.”

Search for a book

To search for a book, navigate to the main page or click the search icon in the upper right to toggle the dropdown search menu. You can query books by title, author, keywords, ISBN, TAMU class code, or biblioQuery seller. Searching by title or keywords will bring up a list of book results matching the query. Clicking on any of those results will bring up a list of editions of that book which, when clicked will navigate to the book listing page for that specific edition. Searching by authors will bring up a list of authors matching that query. Selecting an author will bring up a list of their books which, when clicked, will navigate to the book listing page for that specific book. Searching by ISBN will navigate directly to the book listing page for that ISBN as ISBNs are unique. Searching by TAMU class code (ie. CSCE 315) will bring up a list of books that have been posted by users and tagged as part of that class. When clicked, each book will navigate to its book listing page.

To search by a biblioQuery seller, you must be authenticated with a TAMU Google account. Searching by biblioQuery seller will bring up a list of users which, when selected, will navigate to that user’s profile page where you can view the user’s book listings, reviews, and information.

Buy a book

Once you have found a book through search, the book listing page has many features that can help you find the best price for the book and make sure you get the right book. The top section of the page contains book information, such as ISBN, title, author, publisher, edition number, description, and a cover image. By clicking on the cover image, you can view a Google Books preview of the book *(if one exists) or a link to a PDF preview of the book *(if one exists).

The bottom half of the page contains price information for the book. BiblioQuery pulls price data from 4 sources: booksrun.com, biblioQuery sellers, a search of openlibrary.org's catalog of free PDFs, and a webscrape of bookfinder.com. Each book listing includes the seller title, rating (if they are a biblioQuery seller), price, a book image preview (if one exists), and a link to buy the book or contact the seller. The contact seller button will appear if the listing is by a biblioQuery seller and will take you to the chat page where you can communicate with the seller to negotiate buying the book.

Post a book

To post or sell a book, you must be authenticated with a TAMU Google account. Once you have logged in, click the “Post a Book” button in the upper-left of the navbar. Fill out the form with ISBN, title, author(s), condition of your book, the class you used it for (optional), and an image of the book (optional). If you know the ISBN of the book, you can choose to autofill the title and author(s) of the book by clicking the “Autofill” button. Once you are done, click the “Post book” button at the bottom of the form.

After the book post has been processed, you will be redirected to the book listing page for the book you just posted. To remove your post, scroll down to the table in the bottom half of the page and find the listing with your name on it. Click “remove listing” to remove the listing of the book.

View a user profile

Clicking on a biblioQuery seller name in a book listing or searching for a seller will take you to their profile page. Each biblioQuery seller’s page contains their contact information, their book listings, and reviews left by other users. You can leave a review by clicking the “write a review” button. If you have previously left a review, you will be shown an “edit review” button instead. To delete a review, click the “edit review” button and select “delete”.

Communicate with sellers

To contact a seller using the chat feature, you must be authenticated with a TAMU Google account. Once you have found a book posted by a biblioQuery seller, there are a couple of ways to contact them. There are links to chat with a seller on the book listing page and the seller's profile page and you can search for a seller to chat with by clicking the "new message" icon on the chat page.
